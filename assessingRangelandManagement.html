Detecting What Matters: Gradual vs. Abrupt Change

Older methods like RESTREND assume a stable, linear relationship between climate and vegetation. This can miss or misinterpret abrupt changes from events like fire, severe drought, or crossing an ecological threshold.

Modern methods like TSS-RESTREND are designed to detect these breakpoints, providing a more accurate picture of ecosystem dynamics.

In a study of Australia, TSS-RESTREND identified degradation in 5.25% of pixels‚Äîmore than double the 2.0% found by standard RESTREND.

The Holy Grail: From Correlation to Causation

The ultimate goal is to move beyond finding trends to estimating the precise effect of specific actions. This requires a hierarchy of analytical rigor.

1. Correlation

"Are vegetation and rainfall related?"

2. Attribution

"Is the unexplained trend due to management?"

3. Causation

"What is the exact effect of Action X on Outcome Y?"
