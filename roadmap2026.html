Systems Thinking

"Patterns get you started, numbers decide what survives."

We are moving beyond just "shipping tickets" to understanding the full architectural impact of our choices. In 2026, every design decision must account for scale, failure modes, and long-term maintenance.

Goal: Understand how everything breaks before we build it.

Mental Models for 2026

  • System 1 vs. System 2 Thinking: Move from reactive coding to deliberate design.
  • Second-Order Thinking: "And then what?" when scaling to 4,000 farms.
  • Inversion: Ask how the project could fail (e.g., "What melts the queue?") and defend against it.
  • Map is not the Territory: Adapt to ground truth (data) as we go.

Aligning R&D to Commercial Outcomes

Traditional R&D

"Can we build it?"

Feasibility focus. Long development cycles. Risk of isolation from market reality.

Example: Foundation Models, New Sensors (Theme D)

Business R&D

"Should we build it?"

Desirability & Viability focus. Rapid iteration. Customer-centric validation.

Example: LandLens, AgTrace, PastureKey (Themes A-C)

The 2026 Mindset: "R&D teams are partners in commercialisation, not just creators of technology."

Adopting "Shape Up"

From Tickets to Pitches

We are shifting our operating model to manage expanded scope.

  • The Cycle: 6 weeks of focused work, 2 weeks of cool-down.
  • The Betting Table: Leadership decides based on "Appetite" (time budget).
  • Hill Charts: Tracking progress by "Figuring it out" vs "Making it happen".
  • Workload Balance: "Appetite" creates hard boundaries to prevent burnout.
