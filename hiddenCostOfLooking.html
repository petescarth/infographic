The Hidden Cost of Looking

Quantifying the Financial Impact of Delayed Pasture Assessment

Are Your Eyes Costing You Money?

While most producers regularly check their pastures, the vast majority rely on visual assessment alone. This common practice, despite its widespread use, has a significant, measurable, and costly margin of error.

The Reliance on a Subjective Tool

Industry data reveals a critical gap. Over 85% of producers assess their feedbase, but the method is key. A staggering three-quarters of these assessments are subjective visual appraisals, meaning critical farm decisions are often based on intuition rather than objective data.

The "calibrated eye" is a valuable skill, but it's prone to error, especially in detecting slow decline. An objective tool provides the necessary backup to turn intuition into data-driven confidence.

The "Perception Lag" in Days

The "perception lag" is the time between a real change in pasture and when it's noticed visually. Slower declines, while less dramatic, are harder to see and can go undetected for weeks, silently eroding your feedbase and profitability.

How Long Does It Take to Notice a Decline?

Translating Time Into Money

A 15-day delay in noticing a 500 kg DM/ha pasture deficit creates a significant financial penalty. A producer has two choices: accept the production loss or pay for supplementary feed. Both are costly.

Option A: Unrealised Production

Accepting the deficit means livestock stop gaining, or lose, weight. For a mob of 100 lambs, a 15-day perception lag can wipe out potential gains, representing a direct loss of revenue.
