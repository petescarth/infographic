A Hybrid Method for Determining Drought Onset

Combining vegetation impact with meteorological data to identify, classify, and understand droughts.

Insights and timings are adapted from studies focusing on the Australian continent.

2

The Vegetation Stress Indicator

We calculate the Normalized Difference Fraction Index (NDFI) to measure the balance between healthy and stressed vegetation.

NDFI = ((fNPV - fNPV_base) + fsoil) - fPV  /  ((fNPV - fNPV_base) + fPV + fsoil)

NDFI < 0

Healthy: Green vegetation is dominant.

NDFI > 0

Stressed: Dry vegetation & soil are dominant.

How NDFI Works Across Australia's Pastures

The key is the fNPV_base term. It creates a seasonal baseline, allowing the index to distinguish anomalous drought from predictable seasonal drying.

Northern Monsoonal Pastures

Focuses on deviations from the strong wet/dry cycle. It detects when the landscape dries out faster, earlier, or more severely than the normal dry season.

Drought Triggers Detected:

  • Delayed or failed monsoon onset
  • Mid-wet season "break" period
  • Early end to the wet season

Southern Temperate Pastures

Sensitive to any rapid increase in dry vegetation (NPV), especially outside the typical summer dry-down period.

Drought Triggers Detected:

  • Intense summer heatwaves
  • Prolonged winter/spring dry spells
  • Rapid drying after a wet period
3

Defining Drought Onset

An onset event is a continuous transition from healthy to stressed conditions, followed by a persistence period.

A drought is only confirmed if NDFI remains > 0 for at least 15-20 days.

4

Classifying the Drought

Flash Drought

A rapid-onset event characterized by an exceptionally short development period.

Median Onset in Australia: ~15 days

Conventional Drought

A slow-onset event that develops over a longer timescale.

Median Onset in Australia: ~30 days

Key Finding: Onset Durations Can Be Deceptively Similar

The study found only a small margin separates the median onset times, challenging the idea that conventional droughts are always slow-moving crises.

5

Attributing the Cause

Analyze meteorological driver anomalies to understand *why* the drought occurred. Flash droughts are typically caused by a greater magnitude of anomalies.

The Role of Antecedent Conditions

A crucial finding is that droughts, especially flash droughts, often emerge from near-normal or even wet conditions. They don't necessarily require pre-existing dryness to develop rapidly.

Before Onset

Normal soil moisture & rainfall

During Onset

Intense meteorological anomalies trigger rapid drying

Precipitation Deficit-Driven

The primary cause is a severe lack of rain, leading to dry soil with little water available to evaporate.

  • ↓ Negative Rainfall Anomaly
  • ↓ Negative ET Anomaly

Evaporation-Driven ("Heatwave" type)

High temperatures and solar radiation rapidly pull remaining moisture from the ground.

  • ↓ Negative Rainfall Anomaly
  • ↑ Positive ET Anomaly
  • ↑ Positive Solar Radiation Anomaly
