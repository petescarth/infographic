Reversed Burden of Proof

The responsibility is on the exporter to PROVE their supply chain is clean. Regulators do not need to prove deforestation occurred; the company must provide verifiable evidence that it did NOT.

The "No Mixing" Rule

If a single non-compliant product (e.g., from one farm) is mixed with compliant products, the ENTIRE BATCH is deemed non-compliant. This makes plot-level traceability absolutely critical.

The Critical Nuance: Deforestation vs. Bush Encroachment

Deforestation

The conversion of forest to another land use, such as agriculture. This is what the EUDR aims to prevent.

Bush Encroachment Management

The clearing of invasive native woody shrubs to restore savanna rangeland health. This is an ecologically beneficial practice in Namibia and Botswana, but satellite algorithms can misinterpret it as deforestation.

Global Data Product Accuracy Assessment

Successful EUDR compliance hinges on accurate satellite data. We assessed leading global products for their suitability in the savanna and woodland biomes of Botswana and Namibia. While powerful, all require regional calibration for optimal performance.

Data Product Suitability Comparison

Data Product Deep Dive: Critical Weaknesses for Savannas

GFW (Hansen)

Defines trees as >5m tall, which is ill-suited for many savanna woodlands. Has documented low accuracy in Sub-Saharan Africa.

NASA OPERA

Detects all vegetation loss, not just deforestation. High risk of false positives from natural savanna seasonality (droughts, fires).

ESA WorldCover 10m

Accuracy drops to ~44% in Southern Africa due to major confusion between essential grassland and shrubland classes.

Google Dynamic World

Shows profound spatial disagreements with other products on grass vs. shrub classification, highlighting ambiguity.

Google 'Natural forests'

Excellent starting point, but its >5m height threshold may systematically exclude relevant open-canopy woodlands.

Path Forward: A 4-Step Adaptation Plan

1

Baseline Validation & Refinement

Combine global products (e.g., Google's Natural Forests) with local expert knowledge from partners like SASSCAL and high-resolution imagery to create a legally defensible 2020 baseline that accurately defines "forest" in the regional context.

2

Algorithm Calibration

Develop a region-specific machine learning model by fusing Sentinel-1 (radar) and Sentinel-2 (optical) data. Train it to distinguish genuine deforestation from seasonal variations, fire scars, and sustainable woodland management (debushing).

3

Stakeholder Engagement & UI/UX Localization

Conduct workshops with Namibian and Botswana producers. Ingest key contextual data like farm boundaries and FSC-certified concession polygons to tailor the platform and reduce false positives.

4

Pilot Program Launch

Launch a pilot with select exporters (e.g., members of Namibia's LLPBN) to iterate on the platform's accuracy and generate auditable due diligence statements as a service before a full commercial rollout.

Go-to-Market Strategy

1. Targeted Partnerships

Prioritize formal partnerships with industry bodies (Namibia's LLPBN, Biomass Group) and scientific institutions (SASSCAL) who control access to essential producer and validation data.

2. Phased Market Entry

Focus on Namibia first. The industry is more organized, the commercial need is acute, and the "bush encroachment" problem provides a compelling value proposition. Leverage learnings for a faster Botswana entry.

3. Solution-Oriented SaaS Model

Position the platform as a B2B risk management service, not a technology product. The value proposition: "We provide the guaranteed, auditable proof that secures your access to the EU market."
